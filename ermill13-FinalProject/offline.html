Outside the Classroom

Of course, if I were to only sit on my computer all day programming, I would probably go crazy. Work outside of school has me serving my 4th year with Arizona State University's Polytechnic Residence Hall Association, RHA Poly for short. Of these four years, I spent one as a general council member & the other three as a member of our Executive Board. This board contains six positions crucial in the organization's success and growth within their four pillars of programming (as in events), leadership, advocacy, and networking for those living on campus. As of right now, I am serving my second term as the organization's Executive Director; managing the executive board itself and being the face for our outside professional points of contact such as Deans at ASU, heads of other organizations, local news outlets, and others.

For fun, video games have always been a staple part of my life; starting early on with the Playstation 1 (which was my father's) & the original GameBoy, with Pokemon being the first game I myself ever actually "owned". Those initial impressions stuck with me, transitioning from console to console up till today where I frequently play games on my PC & nintendo switch; enjoying new installations for classic single-player series such as legend of zelda or pokemon alongside fantastic multiplayer experiences with my friends such as Final Fantasy XIV. When I feel like getting some fresh air, I really enjoy going on walks (especially in a forest near my home in Illinois). In the past, I was extremely physically active, but due to old injuries I'm no longer able to do so. However, I strongly believe in a balance between my very tech centric work/pleasure habits & time away from the screens; making walks outside one of my favorite parts of my day.
