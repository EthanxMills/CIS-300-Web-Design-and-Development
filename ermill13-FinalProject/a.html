Lighthouse

During my freshman yea from Fall 2018 to Spring 2019, I was part of a team of four students at Arizona State University participating in an Augmented/Virtual Reality (AR/VR) programming competition. This competition was non-traditional as all participating teams were chosen AFTER a board of judges reviewed applicant project proposals sent in by teams of varying size wishing to participate.

Skills acquired and utilized throughout the project included C# as our primary language, Unity as the engine for creating our map & data visualization, front end design principles, back-end design, data storage (a MySQL Database with php), and numerous APIs that we had to integrate into our project.

The project spanned from September until the following May with us following the Agile SCRUM Software Development Lifecycle. The team proposal for an application which utilizes crime data to provide user's with a navigational app that directs accounting for crime statistics was accepted. The application would eventually include crime data from the entirety of the United States & would created routes for users getting them to their destination while avoiding certain "danger zones". After the project was completed, the team attended a final demonstration day where many industry partners of ASU were invited; including important tech figures such as engineers/leads from Boeing, and officers with the Department of Defense.
